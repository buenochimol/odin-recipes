Bolognese!

What is a Bolognese?

Bolognese is a rich, hearty Italian meat sauce typically made from ground beef (or a mix of beef and pork), tomatoes, onions, garlic, carrots, and celery. Simmered for hours, it results in a flavorful sauce that’s often served with pasta like tagliatelle or fettuccine. It’s a comforting and savory dish that pairs perfectly with a side of Parmesan cheese.

Ingredients:

  • For the Meat Sauce:
    • 1 tbsp olive oil
    • 1 onion (chopped)
    • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
    • 1 carrot (finely chopped)
    • 1 celery stalk (finely chopped)
    • 1 lb ground beef (or a mix of beef and pork)
    • 1 cup red wine (optional)
    • 2 cups crushed tomatoes
    • 2 tbsp tomato paste
    • 1 cup milk or heavy cream
    • 1 tsp dried oregano
    • Salt and pepper to taste
  • For the Pasta:
    • 1 lb pasta (traditionally tagliatelle, fettuccine, or pappardelle)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Vegetables:
    • Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat.
    • Add chopped onions, garlic, carrot, and celery. Cook until the vegetables are softened, about 5–7 minutes.
  2. Cook the Meat:
    • Add the ground beef (or beef and pork mixture) to the pan. Brown the meat, breaking it apart with a spoon.
  3. Add Liquids:
    • If using, pour in the red wine and cook until it reduces by half, about 5 minutes.
    • Stir in the crushed tomatoes and tomato paste. Bring the sauce to a simmer.
    • Reduce the heat and let it simmer gently for 1–1.5 hours, stirring occasionally.
  4. Add Milk or Cream:
    • Stir in the milk or heavy cream, and cook for another 10–15 minutes to smooth the sauce.
  5. Prepare the Pasta:
    • While the sauce is simmering, cook your pasta according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  6. Combine and Serve:
    • Toss the cooked pasta with the bolognese sauce, ensuring the pasta is well coated.
    • Serve with grated Parmesan cheese and fresh basil (optional).
